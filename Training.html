PLAY SCHOOL

About Play School:

Play School aims to encourage a child to wonder, to think, to feel and to imagine. Play is essential to development because it contributes to the cognitive, physical, social, and emotional well-being of children.

Play School aims to extend the child's interest and it encourages participation.

Each program contains a story, some songs (both traditional and new) and a variety of play ideas with things to make and do.

Benefits of Play School:

Most parents give their children plenty of attention in terms of looking after their basic needs and showering them with toys to entertain them. In play schools, however, children are provided with the 'right' toys, i.e. those appropriate to their stage of development. Also, their play is guided so that it becomes a learning experience. Play schools also teach children to recognize their own possessions. Soon a child will learn to identify his schoolbag, learn the mealtime routine that involves opening a lunchbox, folding a napkin and putting everything back after the meal is over and so on. Now this may not seem like a great achievement, but by the standards of toddler behaviour, it is. Children are not born with these skills, they have to develop them. And this is where the play school steps in to help parents out.

Play schools prepare children for their entry into formal school. The first day of school can be quite traumatic for a child. Suddenly, he / she is thrust into a strange world from the warm cocoon that is home. At the age of three or four when the child goes to school for the first time, a lot is expected of him / her. Bawling children, clinging to their parents on the first day of school is a common sight. Play school helps minimize separation anxiety. Play schools prepare children to face the world, as they are more self-confident and independent, they have improved interpersonal skills and better vocabulary.

Playschool provides a foundation for learning both socially and academically that will help your child succeed in elementary school. Playschool promotes language and cognitive skills.
